West Bank

Valley of Kings

The Valley of Kings was used to bury the pharoahs along with their family and high up royal nobles. There are 63 tombs and it holds Pharaohs from Thutmos I to Ramses XI.

Tomb of Ramesses III

Ramesses III was the second pharoah in the Twentieth Dynasty. He led the battle against invaders called "the Sea Peoples" who caused destruction to other places. He came out victorous because he had strong military strategies.

Tomb of Ramesses IV

Ramesses IV was the third pharoah in the Twentieth Dynasty and was the son of Ramesses III. He implemented a building program to increase the size of the workforce and buildings.

Tomb of Ramesses IX

Ramesses IX is the either pharoah in the Twentieth Dynasty and is the grandson of Ramesses III. He was the longest reigning pharoah after Ramesses III and Ramesses XI.

Valley of Queens

The Valley of Queens was used to bury queens, princes, princesses, and high ranking nobles. During 1560 BC to 1130 BC, burials and tombs were created.

Tomb of Prince Amenherkhepshef

Amen Khopshef, or Amenherkhepshef, was the ninth son of Ramesses III.

Tomb of Nefertari

Nerfertari, or Nefertiti, was the Queen during the Eighteenth Dynasty. She is known for her radical religous policy.

Mortuary Temple of Hatshepsut

This was built during the reign of Pharaoh Hatshepsut in the Eighteenth Dynasty. It contains three terraces and Hatshepsut's tomb.

Mortuary Temple of Ramesses III

This huge temple is known for its walls that are inscribed with the stories of how Sea People were defeated by Ramesses III. .

The Ramesseum

This is a funerary temple for Ramesses II. The walls depict scenes of the Battle of Kadesh, the Syrian wars, and the Festival of Min.

The Colossi of Memnon

This contains two huge statues of the Pharaoh Amenhotep III. Originally these stood in front of the largest temple, the Mortuary Temple of Amenhotep III, but it was destroyed.
